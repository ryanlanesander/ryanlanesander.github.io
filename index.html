Nickelodeon - Building the Noggin App

Producer from 2021-2024

As a creative producer at Noggin, I worked with Nickelodeon characters like Dora, Chase, Skye, Marshall (and all the other PAW pups!), and the Bubble Guppies! To make them come alive, I worked in programs like Wrike and Sheets to build timelines & budgets with our project managers and executives, Adobe Creative Cloud & Figma to make sketches, user flows and prototypes, and Unity to build our interactive experiences. My collaborators used programs like Maya, Nuke, Blender and ToonBoom to create the magical Noggin work we made. It was so fun and I loved every minute of directing such a talented collection of artists, from designers & animators to coders & compositors!

There I worked under the wonderful Emma Assin, from whom I learned so much about how to make great kids games--and simple, effective games overall! For a quick sample of some of the experiences we made together, check out the video she put together of some things we made over the years. For an in-depth look, check out my projects tab!

The food show in there was even nominated for an emmy award!

pocket.watch - Directing & Creating Mixed-Media Series

Producer, Writer & Director from 2019-2021

During my time at pocket.watch, I was producing, writing, directing, editing, VFX-making, prop-securing and a million other things, too! It was a true joy to spend the second step of career so busy getting so much experience—I feel very fortunate to have been trusted with so much responsibility so early. It paid off, as the show I produced in my time there The Do Crew came in on-time and on-budget, delivering 24 quarter-hour episodes produced from start to finish in under a year! Not bad for a team of 5!

Just before leaving pocket.watch, I had the pleasure of developing another mixed-media project called Captain Future Pirate for the company, which went on to greenlight after my departure and ultimately sold to Peackock and Amazon Prime for their Ryan and Friends service. Kudos to Derek and the team for bringing it to the finish line!

Adult Swim - Network Development Deal

Creator, Writer & Director from 2019-2020

I started the sketch comedy group Big Game Home Video with my friends Drew Mullins and Julian Lipoff in 2019 (later joined by Steve Marcario and Ryder Chasin), originally just seeing the channel as a way to make things we thought were unique and funny. Then, after we really hit our stride and had several videos under our belt we decided to pitch Adult Swim through their Development Meeting livestream. It was free, and we liked what we were making, so why not give it a shot right? Leading the team, I called in with a pilot I cut together and some other materials—15 minutes later we were on the path to our first development deal! Of over 1000+ pitches, we were only 1 of 3 to get a development deal from Adult Swim which culminated in 2 pilot scripts that we successfully sold to them. This was such a unique experience! Looking back, I’m convinced that it helped me and my team see ourselves as the professional level creatives that we are today, working for places like Paramount, Netflix, Sesame Street and more.

Static Free Media - Marketing, Promotions and Originals

Producer, Writer & Editor from 2017-2019

Static Free Media is a small company with big clients. That’s because the creatives who are in this place’s network made the legendary promos that branded Nickelodeon as the place for kids on TV. Led by co-founder Scott Webb, the team at Static Free and I made podcasts, branding decks, animations and other promotional media for clients like Pinna, Wubbanub, Hasbro, pocket.watch & Nickelodeon. During my time there, I got to direct voice actors not just work with them. I got to write scripts and not just review them. I learned what went into making budgets, and then helped to track them. What a rare and special opportunity that was for sparkly, brand-new producer at that time! The trust Scott put in me to handle all of that responsibility was just the thing I needed. As soon at I set foot in the larger companies I’d eventually work for, I knew I was ready for it. Plus, the many, many after-hours conversations about what makes great kids media from the person who branded Nickelodeon. Working there was awesome; I will forever cherish those days!
