# Mother Teresa - A Life Rightly Ordered, Built Moment-by-Moment Out of Patient, Compounding Yeses
### Marian Consecration Day 14 - March 8th, 2026

Reading about St. Teresa of Calcutta yesterday I was reminded of how throughout my vocational year with Shalom in 2025, St. Teresa was always “asking to be my friend”. 🙂 By this I mean that anytime there were pictures of her, statues, prayer cards, etc. they always stood out to me; people often shared quotes of her with me at critical moments; she would come to mind when I was in prayer often. From the time I was young I always admired her, so this was a very cool experience!

Naturally when we were in Rome, I went to her mission there! It was incredible. The sisters there were people of extraodinary peace. And yet, I was amazed how _normal_ I felt at the Missionaries of Charity's home! Even though it was so plain and simple, so different from places where I’ve lived, I felt like ti fit in. Why did I feel at home in a place so different from every home I've ever known?

Reflecting back now, this is the best explanation I can come up with: From the relationships they had with one another, to the ones they had with guests, to the way they decorated the rooms, to the choices they made about what to go without (there was a _tangible_ a lack of chairs there!), it felt clear to me that all the sisters were living, in a way, the same life that looked something like this:

They pray, they listen, God speaks, they say "yes", they go do as He asks. This series of "compounding yeses" made their lives rightly-ordered, and it showed. It rippled out even to the way their living space looked! The few things they had were not just "enough to get by"; they were also marked, in my view, by a distinct sense that the beds, the desks, the rooms--they were all exactly where they shoule be. When I consider thier life of prayer, it only makes sense: the One Who decides what good order is was basiaclly their interior designer!

How does this relate to my process of consecrating myself to Mary?

Mary embodied this process of "compounding yeses" to the promptings of God perfetly. She is the shining example of Christian life! As wonderful as the Missioaries of Charity are, Mary perfected this sort of life. 

Now, like Mother Teresa and her sisters, I want to give my life to Mary too. In this way, I can learn to say "yes" over and over to God's will in my life, and build one that is beautifully ordered in just the right ways.

_**Prayer**: Mary my mother, please teach me to seek God in all that I do like you and like St. Teresa and her daughters--and remind me to say yes to whatever He asks of me._
