Studies on Gaming as a Child

Here are some useful links to the talks, videos and literature on how modern video games are affecting kids.

Lootboxes and Relation to Gaming Habits

Academic Analysis of Loot Box Prevalence (U of York)

January 2020

University of York look at how many kids’ games have loot boxes. Ultimately they find that “Loot boxes appear to be prevalent in video games that are deemed suitable for children, especially on mobile platforms.”

Children's Commissioner Analysis of Kids and Gaming, Weighing Pros and Cons

October 2019

Children’s Commissioner (UK) interviewed dozens of kids about their thoughts on gaming. They tended to like the social and sense-of-accomplishment aspects of gaming, but of course things like bullying and frustrations from losing were less popular. Also prominently featured in the article: kids feel like they waste money on chance-based gameplay improvements (player packs in FIFA, loot boxes in Fortnite, microtransactions in several games).

Calls for Regulation of Loot Boxes

September 2019

University of York finds loot boxes are having a significant impact on kids while they game, and they go on to recommend regulation of loot boxes as a form of gambling.

Dr. Jamie Madigan on the Gambling of Loot Boxes

June 2019

Dr. Jamie Madigan talks to Rep. Chris Lee (HI) and Dr. David Zendle of York St. John University about legislating loot boxes as gambling and the existing research on loot boxes’ similarities to and differences from gambling, respectively. Great podcast, donate to his efforts here!
